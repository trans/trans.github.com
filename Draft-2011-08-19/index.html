Economics...

An economy that measures vitality by the quantity of jobs is an economy in decline.

Since human beings, as individuals, are finite in physical extent, so to are the sums of them. Therefore a finite limit exists for the total potential consumption of a populace. Any economy is limited to a local maxima by factors of consumption, which include the limits of the consumer. The factors of limitation for a human being include a 16 hour daily activity, limits in calorie and medicinal consumption, which if over-consumed can reduce other factors of consumption,

Prior to maximum consumption, there are a set of lower consumption levels characterized by economic instability in which the future is being sold to fund the consumption of the past. This can only happen for a limited period of time (or at extremely low levels), after which the system will necessarily implode.

As Jesus said when he and his disciples were caught in the fields harvesting corn to eat on the Sabbath, "The Sabbath was made for Man, not Man for the Sabbath."

Ultimately we seek the economy that best serves the needs of the people. Only a sustainable economy can provide this. An economy where resources are used at a predictable rate, where people both have time to work and leisure in greater measure.

You start to dream. Of a new color or texture. Of a new sheen or glossy smudge.

There on the other-side of the documented moon, lies the bounty. The harvest to end all harvests. The boundless bloom, that groundless gloom, that awaits all men.
